Introduction to Mahatma Gandhi

Full Name = Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi

DOB = 2 October 1869

Mahatma Gandhi, also known as the "Father of the Nation" in India, was a visionary leader who played a crucial
role in India’s struggle for independence from British rule. Born on October 2, 1869, in Porbandar, Gujarat, he
became a global icon for his philosophy of nonviolence (Ahimsa) and truth (Satya). Through his unique method of
Satyagraha (nonviolent resistance), he led numerous movements against colonial rule, inspiring millions to fight
for freedom peacefully.

Gandhi’s influence extended beyond India, shaping civil rights movements across the world, including those led
by Martin Luther King Jr. and Nelson Mandela. His principles of simplicity, self-discipline, and social justice
continue to inspire generations. His birthday, October 2nd, is observed as the International Day of
Non-Violence, highlighting his lasting impact on the world.
